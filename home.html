Located in the southwestern region of Cameroon, West Africa, the Forest of Limbe is a unique and fascinating ecosystem that deserves to be explored. Here's a brief description of this remarkable forest:

Waza Waterfall: Located in the Waza National Park, this waterfall is a popular tourist destination. The waterfall drops from a height of about 20 meters and creates a stunning pool of crystal-clear water.

The Forest of Cameroon is situated in the Mount Cameroon Biosphere Reserve, approximately 40 kilometers northwest of the city of Limbe. The forest is nestled in the foothills of Mount Cameroon, the highest mountain in West Africa, which creates a unique microclimate. The forest's elevation ranges from 200 to 500 meters above sea level, and its climate is characterized by high temperatures, high humidity, and moderate rainfall.
